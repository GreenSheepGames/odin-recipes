Onigiri

Onigiri are yummy stuffed rice balls. In Japan, some kids get decorated onigiri in their lunchtime bento boxes that are almost too cute to eat! Centuries ago, the warriors called samurai carried onigiri for a quick, portable meal on the go. Extra onigiri can be wrapped in cling wrap and stored in the fridge for a few days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • bowl of water
  • tuna salad or egg salad for filling
  • veggies, lunch meat, and other food for decoration
  • nori seaweed
  • soy sauce

Steps:

  1. Wet your hands with the water, and then scoopup some rice to form a small ball.
  2. Make a dent in the middle of the ball.
  3. Put some filling in the dent.
  4. Close up the rice around the filing and lightly squeeze it to form a ball or triangle. The shape doesn't need to be perfect.
  5. Get a grownup to cut the seaweed and other food until little shapes that you can use to decorate your onigiri. There's no wrong way to do it - get creative!

    Dip an onigiri in a saucer of soy sauce and enjoy!
